The '''2012–13 Scottish Football League First Division''', also known as the 2012–13 Irn Bru Scottish Football League First Division for sponsorship reasons, is the 18th season of the Scottish First Division and the 107th season of a second-tier football league in Scotland. [[Ross County F.C.|Ross County]] are the current champions.

[[Ross County F.C.|Ross County]] were promoted to the [[Scottish Premier League]] as champions, while [[Dunfermline Athletic F.C.|Dunfermline Athletic]] were relegated from the SPL to this division.

Two sides were relegated from this division at the end of the [[2011–12 Scottish First Division|2011–12]] season. [[Queen of the South F.C.|Queen of the South]], who finished bottom, and [[Ayr United F.C.|Ayr United]] in the play-offs.  They were replaced by [[2011–12 Scottish Second Division|Second Division]] champions [[Cowdenbeath F.C.|Cowdenbeath]], and [[Dumbarton F.C.|Dumbarton]] as winners of the promotion play-offs. 

On 13 July, the [[Newco Rangers]] club were admitted to the Third Division. As a result, the playoff losing finalist [[Airdrie United]] were promoted to the First Division to fill the gap caused by [[Dundee F.C.|Dundee]] replacing [[Rangers F.C.|Rangers]] in the SPL.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/18862114|title=Rangers: Airdrie United and Stranraer are promoted|work=BBC Sport|publisher=BBC|date=16 July 2012|accessdate=16 July 2012}}</ref>

==Results==
Teams play each other four times in this league. In the first half of the season each team plays every other team twice (home and away) and then do the same in the second half of the season, for a total of 36 games
