The '''2012–13 Scottish Football League Second Division''' (also known as the 2011–12 Irn Bru Scottish Football League Second Division for sponsorship reasons) is the 18th season in the current format of 10 teams in the third-tier of Scottish football. [[Cowdenbeath F.C.|Cowdenbeath]] are the current champions.

Two sides were promoted from the [[2011–12 Scottish Second Division|2011–12]] competition.  [[Cowdenbeath F.C.|Cowdenbeath]] as champions, and [[Dumbarton F.C.|Dumbarton]] as winners of the promotion play-offs. They were replaced by [[Queen of the South F.C.|Queen of the South]], who finished bottom of the [[2011–12 Scottish First Division|First Division]], and [[Ayr United F.C.|Ayr United]], who were relegated through the first division play-offs. [[Stirling Albion F.C.|Stirling Albion]] were relegated from this division after finishing bottom, and they were replaced by [[2011–12 Scottish Third Division|Third Division]] winners [[Alloa Athletic F.C.|Alloa Athletic]].  

[[Albion Rovers F.C.|Albion Rovers]] finished in 9th place, but were able to stay up as a result of winning the promotion play-offs. [[Stranraer F.C.|Stranraer]], who had lost to Albion Rovers in the play-off final, were promoted to the Second Division after the decision was made for Rangers fc to start in the Third Division.<ref name = "newco"/> Stranraer replaced [[Airdrie United]], who will take up the space in the First Division.<ref name = "newco">{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/18862114|title=Rangers: Airdrie United and Stranraer are promoted|work=BBC Sport|publisher=BBC|date=16 July 2012|accessdate=16 July 2012}}</ref>

==Results==
Teams play each other four times in this league. In the first half of the season each team plays every other team twice (home and away) and then do the same in the second half of the season, for a total of 36 games
