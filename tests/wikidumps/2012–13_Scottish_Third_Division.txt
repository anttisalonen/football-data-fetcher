The '''2012–13 Scottish Football League Third Division''' (also known as the 2012–13 Irn Bru Scottish Football League Third Division for sponsorship reasons) is in its 18th season in the current format of 10 teams in the fourth tier of Scottish football. [[Alloa Athletic F.C.|Alloa Athletic]] are the current champions. Following the liquidation of their old company, [[Rangers F.C.|Rangers]] have made their debut in this division, having previously played in the SPL.

[[2011–12 Scottish Third Division|2011–12]] champions [[Alloa Athletic F.C.|Alloa Athletic]] were promoted from this division, and were replaced by [[Stirling Albion F.C.|Stirling Albion]], who finished bottom of the [[2011–12 Scottish Second Division|Second Division]].

Another promotion spot was available through the promotion play-offs, however this was won by second division side [[Albion Rovers F.C.|Albion Rovers]], who thus stayed in the Second Division.

Stranraer were elevated to the Second Division on July 13th after the SFL clubs all voted that [[Rangers F.C.|Rangers]] were to be put in the Third Division. 

There was no relegation from this division, as this is the lowest in the Scottish league.
